## The Problem: Decisions Without Memory

Across organisations, critical decisions are often:
- Made in meetings, emails, or slide decks
- Justified by temporary constraints or assumptions
- Forgotten once implementation starts

When context is lost:
- New teams cannot understand *why things are the way they are*
- Fixing or modernising systems becomes risky and expensive
- Mistakes are repeated
- Compliance, security, and auditability suffer
- AI systems and automation lack reliable historical context

## Our Core Idea

**Architecture decisions should be treated as first-class records.**

Just like financial records, legal documents, or policies:
- Architecture decisions deserve structure
- They deserve traceability
- They deserve to survive personnel and technology changes

This project promotes the idea that:
> *Well-documented decisions enable better future decisions.*

---

## About Architecture Decision Records (ADRs)

The concept of **Architecture Decision Records (ADRs)** originated in software engineering â€” but the principle applies far beyond software.

At its core, an ADR answers:
- What decision was made?
- In what context?
- What alternatives were considered?
- Why was this option chosen?
- What consequences were expected?

This project **extends that thinking** to:
- Enterprise IT architecture
- Digital policy and governance
- Security and compliance decisions
- Cloud, data, and platform strategies
- Operational and organisational design choices

ADRs are a **starting point**, not the limit.
